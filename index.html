Methodology

This analysis evaluates public asset exposure to flooding under current and future conditions using floodplain datasets developed by Rutgers University and the New Jersey Department of Environmental Protection (NJDEP).

The 2025 flood scenario represents the 1% annual chance (100-year) floodplain derived from Rutgers’ modified statewide FEMA Flood Hazard Area layer, integrating effective and preliminary FEMA Flood Insurance Rate Maps and modeled water-surface elevations under current conditions.

The 2050 flood scenario represents the 1% annual chance (100-year) floodplain plus three feet of additional flood elevation, developed by Rutgers in consultation with NJDEP to align with New Jersey’s Inland Flood Protection Rule.

Public asset locations were buffered by 100 feet to account for building footprints, access constraints, and localized service disruption beyond a single point location. Assets were classified as exposed if their buffered area intersected either floodplain.

Flood scenarios represent water-level–based flooding only and do not include rainfall-driven runoff, stormwater ponding, or sewer system impacts.
